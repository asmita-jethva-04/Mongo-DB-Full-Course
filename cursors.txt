Cursors :- MongoDB does not return all documents at once. Instead, it returns a cursor, which points to the result set.A query may return thousands or millions of documents.Loading everything at once is slow and uses lot of memory.A cursor loads data in batches, making it faster and efficient. 

1 :- count() - count the total records
     ex :- db.products.find({'price':{$gt : 250}}).count();

2 :- limit() - display the limited records
     ex :- db.products.find({'price':{$gt : 250}}).limit(5);

3 :- skip() - skip the records
     ex :- db.products.find({'price':{$gt : 250}}).limit(5).skip(2);

4 :- sort() - sorting the records
     ex :- db.products.find({'price':{$gt : 250}}).limit(5).sort({price:1});
     (NOTE :- 1 = assecending & -1 = Desscending)
